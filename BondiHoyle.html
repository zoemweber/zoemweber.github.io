A Stufy of Bondi–Hoyle–Lyttleton Accretion in Star Clusters

The purpose of this study was to determine how BHL affects a star’s ability to accrete ambient gas. Through the use of FLASH we created a three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation of clusters of stars embedded in a supersonic, uniform density wind of ambient gas. We Aimed to show how the accretion rate of a star cluster depends on the number density of stars, the separation between them and the equation of state held by the gas.

Analytical data concluded that accretion taking place in globular clusters will increase as the number of cluster members rises and the distance between stars becomes smaller. For globular clusters that have a smaller distance of separation between stars, the mass accretion rate can be calculated as a whole rather than by the addition of each individual star.
